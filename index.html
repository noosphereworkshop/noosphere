Noosphere: Tactile Sensing for General Purpose Robots

  • When: Monday, July 15 or Friday, July 19 (to be confirmed)
  • Where: Robotics Science and Systems (RSS) 2024, Delft, Netherlands

As put forward in early demonstrations with PR2 Robots, and more recently in the low-cost ALOHA project, the technologies for building general-purpose robots are ready, as existing robots can perfectly be teleoperated to achieve a variety of daily tasks. Consequently, the current main limitation for general-purpose robots would be (artificial) intelligence and low-cost availability of hardware. However, within the community of tactile sensing, the argument that tactile sensing is highly necessary is still brewing; After all, all animals (and humans) are endowed with tactile sensing from very early on.

Despite this, these communities evolve separately with tactile sensing practitioners mostly catching up with non tactile sensing focused approaches.

As such, this workshop brings together expert roboticists interested in general-purpose robotics, in a thrilling one-day event to discuss the following research questions:

  • Is tactile sensing just an "add-on", or a fundamentally relevant capability for learning-based robots?
  • What's the cost of ignoring tactile sensing?
  • How should tactile sensing be included in learning-based robots,
    to improve learning and adaptability?
